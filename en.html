Background

The Story Behind Trah Manshur

Cinangka Village, Ciampea District, Bogor Regency — In 1978, this area became a centre for smelting used batteries, producing hundreds of thousands of cubic metres of lead (B3) waste. The soil became contaminated with Pb (Plumbum) toxins; the water there was polluted — if consumed, it could cause mental disabilities.

This situation alarmed local residents. In 2014, the Minister of Environment under President SBY's administration personally visited Cinangka Village.

It was from this deep concern that Yayasan Trah Manshur was born — which fortunately possesses a natural spring uncontaminated by such waste — to be distributed to affected communities, including Islamic boarding schools and mosques.

Since 2013, under the leadership of Azhar Siroj, the foundation has helped address clean water issues in 3 villages: Cinangka, Ciampea, Bogor. Beyond clean water, the foundation also supports residents in farming activities.

Alhamdulillah, we have distributed clean water through pipe connections to residents, mosques, prayer halls, and Islamic boarding schools. The Village Official of Cinangka, Mr. Taufik Hidayat, has praised the clean water management by Yayasan Trah Manshur for assisting the government in providing clean water to affected communities.

Therefore, we invite the servants of Allah to participate in the social activities of Yayasan Trah Manshur.
